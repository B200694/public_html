Help



Protif allows you to interact with both our local database and NCBI's protein database to explore protein sequence conservation and motifs across taxonomic groups.


To use the tool, simply enter the taxonomic group and protein you are interested in and click the search button. Searches that are not found in the local database will be queried from NCBI.


Following a search, you will be able to view the results in table format and are given the option to generate a conservation plot or scan for protein sequence motifs. Don't forget that the sequence column in the search results table is scrollable so you can view the whole sequence!


Two example datasets from the local database are provided:

  • glucose-6-phosphatase (G6Pase) in Aves (birds)
  • acetyl-CoA carboxylase (ACCase) in Arabidopsis (thale cress)

We recommend using the first example set to explore the conservation analysis tool, and the second to explore the motif scanning tool.



NOTE: Critical to the maintenance and synthesis of cellular membranes, ACCase has long been a prominent herbicide target, but widespread resistance is threatening food security globally. For more information, check out Moa Technology, an Oxford-based company pioneering the development of novel herbicides.
